The Whispering Alms

MAY 14, 1924

The rain did not fall so much as it surrendered.

Elias stood at the edge of the copper docks, his boots sinking slightly into the brine-soaked timber. The air here smelled of ozone and rusted dreams, a scent he had grown to associate with the inevitable. Behind him, the city of NarraForge hummed with the sound of a thousand ticking clocks, each one a heartbeat in a body of brass and steam.

He adjusted the collar of his coat, the fabric heavy with the morning's mist. He wasn't looking for trouble—trouble in this city usually found you long before you had the chance to look—but he was looking for a man who didn't want to be found. A man whose name was etched in the back of a silver pocket watch currently burning a hole in Elias's pocket.

flare

"You're early," a voice rasped from the shadows of a stack of crates.

Elias didn't turn. He knew that voice. It was like sand moving over silk. "And you're still hiding, Thorne. Some things never change."

A figure stepped into the flickering light of a nearby gas lamp. Thorne looked older, his face a map of regrets, but his eyes still held that sharp, predatory glint. "I prefer to call it 'selective visibility.' Now, do you have it?"

Elias reached into his pocket. The silver felt cold against his palm, a stark contrast to the humid air. He pulled out the watch and held it up, the moonlight catching the intricate engravings on its face. "I have it. But I want answers first. Why did she give this to me?"
