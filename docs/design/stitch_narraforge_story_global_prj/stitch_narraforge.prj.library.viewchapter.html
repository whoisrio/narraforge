Chapter 04 • Narrative Script

The rain did not fall so much as it surrendered.

Elias stood at the edge of the copper docks, his boots sinking slightly into the brine-soaked timber. The air here smelled of ozone and rusted dreams, a scent he had grown to associate with the inevitable.
Behind him, the city of NarraForge hummed with the sound of a thousand ticking clocks, each one a heartbeat in a body of brass and steam. He wasn't looking for trouble—trouble in this city usually found you.
"You're early," a voice rasped from the shadows of a stack of crates.
"And you're still hiding, Thorne. Some things never change."
